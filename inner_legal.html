SUMMARY


In an era marked by the rapid dissemination of information, the prevalence of disinformation has emerged as a pressing social problem. To address this challenge, we aim to develop a "factbased" chatbot that serves as a reliable source of information. By leveraging the capabilities of this chatbot, we seek to counteract misinformation and empower individuals and organizations to make informed decisions. Our objective is to ensure the quality of information provided by chatbots, thereby offering users credible answers sourced from reliable sources. Moreover, we recognize the explicit interest from various market segments, including political candidates, civil society organizations, and academics, in harnessing the potential of such technology. Through this feasibility study, we aim to explore the viability of developing and implementing fact-based LLMs in chatbot form to address these critical needs and opportunities. findings to conclude the overall feasibility and potential implications.

CHALLENGE


Fact-checking legal documents is crucial to ensure accuracy and reliability. Here are some steps you can follow:

  • Read the Entire Document: Read the entire document thoroughly to understand its purpose and context.
  • Check Dates and Names: Verify all dates, names, addresses, and other identifying information for accuracy.
  • Verify Legal Citations: Check all legal citations, references, and footnotes to ensure they are accurate and up-to-date.
  • Review Legal Terms and Definitions: Confirm that all legal terms and definitions used in the document are correct and appropriate for the context.
  • Cross-Check References: Verify all cross-references within the document to ensure consistency and accuracy.
  • Review Legal Authority: Verify the legal authority cited in the document, such as statutes, regulations, case law, etc.
  • Confirm Formatting and Structure: Ensure that the document is formatted correctly and follows the appropriate structure for its type (e.g., contract, brief, memorandum).
  • Check for Grammar and Spelling Errors: Review the document for any grammar, spelling, or typographical errors.
  • Consult with Subject Matter Experts: If necessary, consult with subject matter experts, such as lawyers or legal scholars, to ensure accuracy and completeness.
  • Final Review: Conduct a final review of the document to ensure that all necessary changes have been made and that it is ready for distribution or filing.

INNOVATION


The appropriate data sources for training and evaluating the LLM chat-bot model must prioritize quality and diversity. Well-edited books and literature provide structured language examples, while scientific papers offer accurate, specialized content. Reliable news outlets and educational materials like Wikipedia contribute to a mix of current affairs and factual knowledge. Despite variability, online forums and social media offer insights into informal language use. Government and legal documents provide authoritative language structures. Specialized fact-checking datasets such as FEVER and LIAR are crucial for refining fact-checking abilities.
